Description

The snow leopard is a large grey cat with black spots similar to that of an Asia Leopard. Their adult body length ranges from 29-51 inches with a tail of 27-39 inches. Standing at 20-25 inches, their body weights range from 77-155 pounds.

Habitat

Found primarily in Central Asia amongst highland habitats. Occuring from Turkistan in the west through Pakisatan and India in the east. Living as high as 19,685 feet in elevation, snow leopards tend to migrate to lower elecations in the winter.

Cubs

With pregnancies ranging between 93 and 103 days, snow leopards give birht to two cubs on average, but can have as many as five. Reaching maturity around two years old, snow leopards can live up to 19 years in captivity. Those amongst the wilderness would have considerably fewer years.

Prey

As solitary predators, snow leopard typically rest during the day and feed during the early morning or late afternoon.

  • Sheep
  • Musk Deer
